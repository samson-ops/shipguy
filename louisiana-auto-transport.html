Open Car Transport in Louisiana

Open car transport is the most common and affordable way to move a car in or out of Louisiana. If you drive a daily sedan, SUV, or family truck, open transport is almost certainly the right fit.

Vehicles travel on two-level open carriers, the same type of trucks you see Louisiana car dealerships use to move inventory along I-10 and I-12. Open transport is the most common car shipping method across the industry, and it handles the vast majority of vehicle shipments nationwide.

Common scenarios where open transport makes the most sense:

  • Moves between New Orleans and Texas
  • Baton Rouge to Florida relocations
  • Cross-country moves on a budget.
  • Dealer-to-dealer inventory transfers

Open trailers do expose vehicles to weather and road debris during transit. However, most cars are shipped safely on open trailers every day, and damage incidents are statistically rare. Every shipment remains covered by the carrier's insurance, so you are protected even in the unlikely event of an incident.

Enclosed & Expedited Louisiana Auto Transport

Enclosed trailers protect luxury and classic cars during transport by providing full coverage from Gulf Coast rain, hail, UV exposure, and road debris. If you are shipping a restored muscle car, a high-end sports vehicle, or anything with collector value headed to or from New Orleans, Baton Rouge, or a car show, enclosed transport is the way to go.

Enclosed transport costs 30 to 40 percent more than open transport, but that premium buys complete weather protection and added privacy for high-value vehicles. During Louisiana's hurricane season, when Louisiana's tropical storms can impact auto transport safety, enclosed carriers offer an extra layer of peace of mind. For anyone shipping classic, exotic, or luxury cars worth six figures, that premium is easy to justify.

Expedited Louisiana auto transport is the right choice when timelines are tight. Last-minute job relocations, vehicles that must arrive before a specific closing date, or time-sensitive dealer transfers all benefit from faster vehicle pickup windows and more direct routing. Expedited service usually carries a premium over standard delivery dates, but it ensures your car moves on your schedule rather than the carrier's.

What Affects Your Louisiana Car Shipping Quote?

Several factors determine the final number on your Louisiana car shipping cost estimate:

  • Route popularity. High-traffic corridors like New Orleans to Houston see frequent carrier traffic and lower per-mile rates. Rural origins or delivery locations with less truck volume cost more. Transporting to rural areas often incurs higher shipping costs compared to urban areas.
  • Vehicle type and size. Larger or heavier vehicles (full-size SUVs, trucks, lifted rigs) take up more carrier space and add weight, which increases car shipping rates. Provide accurate vehicle details when requesting a quote so pricing reflects reality.
  • Operable vs. inoperable. Non-running vehicles require winch or lift equipment for loading, which adds cost and coordination time.
  • Transport type. Open car transport is usually the lowest-cost option, while enclosed and expedited services increase the overall cost of car shipping.
  • Seasonality. Summer and holidays drive up car shipping prices. Peak seasons can lead to higher prices for auto transport services because carrier availability tightens. Late spring through early summer and the weeks around Mardi Gras tend to be the busiest in Louisiana.
  • Pickup window. Carriers prefer pickup locations near major highways for efficiency. If your address is off a main corridor, expect slightly longer pickup windows.

Two practical ways to save money:

  • Booking early can significantly reduce your car shipping costs. Carriers have more flexibility to accommodate your load when you give them advance notice.
  • Be flexible on pickup and delivery dates when possible. A window of two to three days instead of a fixed date often secures better car shipping rates.

How to Prepare Your Vehicle for Louisiana Auto Transport

Proper preparation protects your vehicle and speeds up both pickup and delivery inspections. A few minutes of effort before the carrier arrives can prevent headaches later.

Clean and document your car. Wash the vehicle so existing dings, scratches, or chips are easy to see. Document your vehicle's condition with photos from all angles, including close-ups of any existing damage, using timestamps. These photos serve as your reference if you ever need to file a claim.

Remove personal items and accessories. Remove fragile items and personal belongings from the vehicle. CCarriers are not liable for items left inside, and loose objects can shift during transit, causing interior damage. Also, remove toll transponders before shipping your vehicle, and take off or secure aftermarket parts like roof racks, antenna toppers, or removable spoilers.

Mechanical readiness checklist:

  • Ensure the car starts, steers, and brakes properly (unless you have booked inoperable service).
  • Keep about a quarter tank of gas in the vehicle. More than that adds unnecessary weight; less can cause issues if the car needs to be repositioned.
  • Check coolant and oil levels before transport to prevent leaks on the carrier.
  • Verify tire pressure is at recommended levels.
  • Disable aftermarket alarms that could drain the battery or trigger during transit.

Taking these steps means the driver can load your car quickly, and you can move through the Bill of Lading inspection without delays.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you ship.

How much does it cost to ship a vehicle?
Your price depends on the distance, vehicle size, transport type (open or enclosed), and how quickly you need it. We give you an upfront, all-in quote with no hidden fees — the number you see is the number you pay.
How long will my shipment take?
Transit time depends on the route and distance. Once your vehicle is dispatched, we share an estimated delivery window and keep you updated from pickup to drop-off. Longer cross-country routes naturally take more time than regional moves.
Is my vehicle insured during transport?
Yes. We only work with licensed, vetted carriers that carry active cargo insurance, and your vehicle is covered while it's on the truck. We're happy to share coverage details before you book.
What's the difference between open and enclosed transport?
Open transport is the most common and most affordable option and is safe for everyday vehicles. Enclosed transport adds extra protection from weather and road debris — a great choice for classic, luxury, or high-value vehicles.
Do I have to pay a deposit upfront?
No. You don't pay until your vehicle is assigned and dispatched to a carrier — so there's no risk in getting a quote and booking your shipment.
Can you ship a vehicle that doesn't run?
Yes. We transport both operable and inoperable (non-running) vehicles — just let us know when you book so we assign a carrier with the right equipment, such as a winch.
