Open Carrier Auto Transport in Alabama

Open car shipping is the most common shipping method in the industry. About 90% of cars shipped use open transport methods, making it the default choice for most Alabama vehicle shipments.

Vehicles travel on multi-car open trailers, the same type you see regularly along I-20, I-65, and I-10. Cars are exposed to weather during transit, but the safety record for open carriers is strong, and incidents of meaningful damage are uncommon.

Open transport works well for daily drivers and most standard vehicles. Think Honda Civics, Ford Explorers, Toyota RAV4s, and similar everyday cars. Open carriers are the most affordable shipping option, and they typically offer the best carrier availability and fastest scheduling for popular Alabama corridors.

Enclosed Auto Transport for Luxury & Classic Cars

Enclosed car transport protects vehicles from the weather and road debris by housing them in a fully enclosed trailer. This is the recommended option for high-value vehicles that warrant extra protection from Alabama's rain, humidity, and highway grit.

Vehicles that benefit from enclosed transportation include a classic car like a 1969 Chevrolet Camaro, luxury cars such as the Porsche 911, exotic cars like Lamborghinis and Ferraris, high-end EVs with sensitive aero components, luxury vehicles with fresh paint, and restored vintage trucks. Enclosed car shipping also suits vehicles with low ground clearance that need careful loading.

Enclosed transport costs 40-60% more than open transport because enclosed carriers ship fewer vehicles per load and often include higher insurance limits and added handling care. For classic vehicles traveling to shows in Birmingham, Huntsville, or along the Gulf Coast, Ship Guy recommends enclosed trailers for peace of mind.

The choice between open and enclosed transport ultimately comes down to the vehicle type and the level of protection you want during transit. For oversized vehicles or those with unusual dimensions, enclosed options also reduce exposure risk.

How to Prepare Your Vehicle for Alabama Auto Transport

Proper preparation helps avoid delays and makes inspections smoother, especially in Alabama's hot, humid conditions. Auto shipping runs more efficiently when vehicles are ready to load without surprises.

Follow these steps before your pickup date:

Wash the car. A clean surface makes it easier to spot and document existing damage during the pre-pickup inspection.

Remove personal items. Personal belongings left in the vehicle are not covered by cargo insurance. Take out everything that isn't permanently attached.

Ensure your gas tank is no more than 1/4 full for transport. This reduces vehicle weight and meets carrier safety requirements.

Check for fluid leaks before vehicle transport. Address any active leaks before pickup to prevent issues during loading and transit.

Disable your vehicle's alarm prior to pickup. A triggered alarm on a moving trailer creates problems for everyone.

Document your vehicle's condition with photos before shipping. Take dated photos of all four sides, the roof, wheels, and close-ups of any existing scratches, dents, or chips. Record the odometer reading.

Remove or secure accessories. Toll tags, roof racks, bike racks, and loose antenna toppers should be removed or firmly secured.

Customers in rural Alabama should consider meeting carriers at a nearby larger parking lot, such as a shopping center near an interstate exit, to simplify loading when local roads are too narrow for a full-size trailer.

How to Save on Alabama Car Shipping Costs

Smart planning can noticeably reduce car shipping costs without sacrificing quality. Here are the most effective strategies.

Book your shipment 1-2 weeks in advance for better rates on common routes. For remote pickups or peak seasons (summer, year-end), aim for 2–3 weeks of lead time. Rates tighten when carriers can plan your vehicle into an existing route rather than making a special trip.

Choose open transport for most standard vehicles. Unless your car qualifies as high-value or is a classic, open carriers offer the best value. Local auto transport services running established corridors pass those efficiencies on to customers.

Offer flexible pickup and delivery dates. A 2–3 day window helps Ship Guy match you with carriers already running similar Alabama routes, which can lower the rate compared to a fixed-date request.

Consolidate when possible. Families or businesses that ship multiple vehicles on the same order may qualify for per-vehicle savings when capacity allows. Al car shipping for fleet moves, this approach benefits regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you ship.

How much does it cost to ship a vehicle?
Your price depends on the distance, vehicle size, transport type (open or enclosed), and how quickly you need it. We give you an upfront, all-in quote with no hidden fees — the number you see is the number you pay.
How long will my shipment take?
Transit time depends on the route and distance. Once your vehicle is dispatched, we share an estimated delivery window and keep you updated from pickup to drop-off. Longer cross-country routes naturally take more time than regional moves.
Is my vehicle insured during transport?
Yes. We only work with licensed, vetted carriers that carry active cargo insurance, and your vehicle is covered while it's on the truck. We're happy to share coverage details before you book.
What's the difference between open and enclosed transport?
Open transport is the most common and most affordable option and is safe for everyday vehicles. Enclosed transport adds extra protection from weather and road debris — a great choice for classic, luxury, or high-value vehicles.
Do I have to pay a deposit upfront?
No. You don't pay until your vehicle is assigned and dispatched to a carrier — so there's no risk in getting a quote and booking your shipment.
Can you ship a vehicle that doesn't run?
Yes. We transport both operable and inoperable (non-running) vehicles — just let us know when you book so we assign a carrier with the right equipment, such as a winch.
