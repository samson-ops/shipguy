Types of Colorado Vehicle Transport Services

Ship Guy offers multiple Colorado car shipping services tailored to different budgets and protection levels. Choosing the right transport type affects your car shipping costs, the transit timeline, and the level of protection your vehicle receives during the move. Here's how each option works.

Open Car Transport in Colorado

Open car transport uses multi-vehicle open carriers - the large trucks you see regularly on I-25 and I-70 hauling eight to ten vehicles at once. Open auto transport is the most affordable option for shipping cars, accounting for roughly 90% of all vehicle shipments nationwide.

This service is ideal for daily drivers, leased vehicles, and standard sedans or SUVs shipped to or from Denver, Colorado Springs, and other metro areas. Vehicles are exposed to weather and road debris during transit, but are secured with industry-standard straps. Open transport costs up to two-thirds less than enclosed transport, which is why most dealership and auction moves in Colorado use open car shipping to keep pricing competitive.

If you're looking for lower cost and broad carrier availability, open transport is the way to go.

Enclosed Auto Transport for High-Value Vehicles

Enclosed car transport is the premium option. Hard-sided or soft-sided enclosed trailer configurations protect vehicles from snow, hail, dust, and road debris - a serious consideration for anyone shipping through Colorado's winter months.

Enclosed transport is recommended for luxury vehicles, sports cars, restored classics, and collector vehicles. These loads often travel to mountain homes, car shows, or warm-weather destinations like Scottsdale. Think of it this way: if you've spent serious money restoring a 1967 Corvette in Boulder, you don't want salt spray from I-70 hitting the paint on its way to an Arizona car show.

Enclosed Colorado shipping typically costs 30 to 60 percent more than open transport, but the trade-off includes higher cargo insurance coverage (often $250,000 to $1,000,000 per load), more careful handling, and full weather protection. Enclosed transport protects vehicles from weather and debris, making it the right call for high-value vehicles.

Motorcycle Shipping in Colorado

Ship Guy coordinates motorcycle shipping using palletized or crated methods inside specialized trailers or enclosed carriers. Handlebars and wheels are secured to prevent movement, which matters especially when shipments travel through Colorado's mountain passes and rougher terrain.

Common use cases include transporting bikes for seasonal riding, buying or selling motorcycles between Colorado Springs and other states, or relocating military personnel's bikes during a PCS move. Pricing for motorcycle shipping is generally lower than for cars, but it still depends on distance, route, and whether open shipping or enclosed shipping is selected.

Specialty and Oversized Vehicle Transport

Ship Guy can also coordinate transport for lifted trucks, dually pickups, small RVs, and work vehicles commonly used in Colorado's construction and outdoor industries. Oversized dimensions, aftermarket modifications, or inoperable conditions may require special equipment - like a liftgate or winch - and will influence the final quote.

Examples include lifted 4x4 trucks moving between Denver and oil and gas regions on the Eastern Plains, or farm equipment headed to auction. Customers should always disclose modifications and exact dimensions when requesting a car shipping quote so Ship Guy can match the right carrier and provide an accurate estimate.

Key Factors That Impact Car Shipping Costs in Colorado

The main pricing factors for Colorado vehicle shipping include:

Distance and route popularity. High-traffic lanes like Denver to Salt Lake City or Denver to Dallas tend to have more carrier availability and competitive pricing.

Vehicle size and weight. A compact sedan costs less to ship than a full-size SUV or lifted pickup.

Operable vs. inoperable. Non-running vehicles require winching or specialized equipment, which adds to the cost.

Open vs. enclosed. Open car transport is cheaper; enclosed is for those who need maximum protection.

Time of year. Summer (May through September) and the year-end moving season are peak periods. Book your shipment during off-peak seasons for lower rates.

Fuel prices. Gas prices directly affect carrier operating costs and are passed along in quotes.

Pickup and delivery location. Shipping to or from dense areas like Denver and Colorado Springs is often cheaper and faster than remote mountain or rural locations, where access fees may apply.

Winter conditions on I-70 and mountain passes can influence both cost and timing. Snow closures, rockslides, and heavy salt use on roads sometimes force route changes or delays. Booking earlier, around peak moving months, helps you save money and secure better availability rating windows.

Preparing Your Vehicle for Colorado Shipping

Proper preparation helps avoid delays and makes inspections easier for both you and the carrier. A few simple steps before pickup day can prevent headaches and protect your interests if anything goes sideways during transit.

A clean vehicle helps document existing damage before transport - this is especially important in Colorado, where hail damage and rock chips are common and can be hard to spot on a dirty surface. Document your vehicle's condition with photos before transport, including close-ups of any existing dents, scratches, or windshield chips. Date your photos so there's no ambiguity if a claim needs to be filed.

Colorado-specific prep tips: if you're shipping during winter, check antifreeze levels and battery health. Cold temperatures at altitude can drain weak batteries, and you want the vehicle ready to start when it arrives at the other end. Note any existing hail damage common to Front Range vehicles.

Pre-Pick Up Checklist

Before the carrier arrives, make sure you've handled these essentials:

Wash the vehicle. Makes pre-transport inspection faster and more thorough.

Remove personal items. Carriers are not licensed to transport personal belongings in vehicles. Leaving items inside can violate carrier policies and affect insurance coverage.

Remove toll tags and disable alarms. Disable alarms and remove toll tags from your vehicle to prevent unnecessary charges or disruptions during transit.

Check tire pressure and fluid levels. Ensure tires are properly inflated, and basic functions work for easier loading and unloading.

Reduce fuel level. Ensure your gas tank is no more than a quarter full. This reduces weight while leaving enough fuel to move the car on and off the trailer.

Fold in mirrors and secure loose parts. Retract antennas, fold mirrors, and remove or secure any loose accessories or aftermarket parts.

Confirm the parking brake works. The vehicle needs to hold position on the trailer during transport.

Keep a spare key. Hand one set to the driver and keep a backup.

Remove personal items from your vehicle before transport - this point is worth repeating because many customers overlook it. Items left inside are not covered by carrier insurance, and excess weight from packed belongings can create liability issues. Check tire pressure and fluid levels before shipping so the vehicle can be loaded and securely transported without mechanical issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you ship.

How much does it cost to ship a vehicle?
Your price depends on the distance, vehicle size, transport type (open or enclosed), and how quickly you need it. We give you an upfront, all-in quote with no hidden fees — the number you see is the number you pay.
How long will my shipment take?
Transit time depends on the route and distance. Once your vehicle is dispatched, we share an estimated delivery window and keep you updated from pickup to drop-off. Longer cross-country routes naturally take more time than regional moves.
Is my vehicle insured during transport?
Yes. We only work with licensed, vetted carriers that carry active cargo insurance, and your vehicle is covered while it's on the truck. We're happy to share coverage details before you book.
What's the difference between open and enclosed transport?
Open transport is the most common and most affordable option and is safe for everyday vehicles. Enclosed transport adds extra protection from weather and road debris — a great choice for classic, luxury, or high-value vehicles.

No. You don't pay until your vehicle is assigned and dispatched to a carrier — so there's no risk in getting a quote and booking your shipment.
Can you ship a vehicle that doesn't run?
Yes. We transport both operable and inoperable (non-running) vehicles — just let us know when you book so we assign a carrier with the right equipment, such as a winch.
