Open Car Transport in Maryland

Open car transport is the most common and affordable way to ship a vehicle in Maryland.

With open car shipping, vehicles travel on open multi-car carriers, the same type of trucks used to deliver new cars from factories to dealerships across the country. These carriers typically hold 7 to 10 vehicles and run consistent routes along high-traffic corridors.

This option is ideal for everyday vehicles-sedans, SUVs, crossovers, and pickups-and works well for most long-distance routes such as Maryland to Texas, Maryland to Georgia, or Maryland to Illinois. Open car shipping is the most affordable option, making it the go-to choice for most customers.

Vehicles on open carriers are exposed to weather conditions and road dust during transit, but drivers secure each car with professional tie-downs and wheel straps. Millions of vehicles are shipped this way every year, and it remains the industry standard for good reason.

Enclosed Auto Shipping for HighValue Vehicles

Enclosed car transport is the premium option for luxury, classic, or specialty vehicles that need extra protection during shipping.

With enclosed transport, vehicles ride inside hard-sided or soft-sided trailers, fully shielded from rain, snow, road debris, and salt spray. This shipping method is particularly valuable during Maryland winters, when road salt on I-95 can cause corrosion on exposed finishes.

For example, if you are shipping a restored 1969 Mustang from Bethesda to Miami, enclosed auto shipping keeps the paint, chrome, and undercarriage protected throughout the trip. Carriers offering enclosed service often provide higher insurance coverage, sometimes up to $1,000,000, compared to standard open transport policies.

Enclosed transport protects vehicles from weather and road debris, but it typically costs 30 to 60 percent more than open car shipping. The trade-off is worth it for high-value cars, where even minor cosmetic damage could result in a significant loss. Enclosed transport is recommended for high-value or luxury vehicles that justify the added investment.

Preparing Your Vehicle for Maryland Car Shipping

Proper preparation protects your vehicle and speeds up both pickup and delivery. Taking 30 minutes before your car is picked up can prevent headaches down the road.

Start by washing the car. A clean vehicle makes it easier to spot and document existing damage before the driver arrives. Clean your vehicle to reveal existing damage so nothing is missed during the pre-shipping inspection.

Remove personal items weighing over 100 lbs from the vehicle. Most carriers prohibit heavy personal belongings because they add weight, shift during transit, and are not covered by the carrier's cargo insurance. Remove personal items, such as gym equipment, boxes, or tools, from the trunk and interior. Ship Guy recommends keeping the cabin and trunk clear of anything that is not permanently attached.

Secure or remove any loose parts, including aftermarket spoilers, bike racks, and retractable antennas. These can catch wind or snag during loading and unloading. Check fluids and battery charge to ensure the vehicle is drivable. Confirm proper tire pressure and keep the fuel tank at about a quarter tank for loading and unloading. A quarter tank provides enough fuel for the driver to maneuver the car on and off the trailer without adding unnecessary weight.t.

Disable any security systems that may interfere during transport, such as aftermarket alarms that could be triggered by trailer vibrations. Remove parking permits or toll passes you do not want misplaced, and provide the driver with a spare key.

Maryland's weather is another reason to document exterior conditions carefully. Winter road salt and summer storms can leave marks that might be mistaken for transport damage if not photographed beforehand. Take photos of your vehicle's condition before shipping, covering all four sides, the roof, and the undercarriage if possible.

Inspection, Documentation, and Delivery in Maryland

The pre-shipping and post-delivery inspections are the most important steps for protecting both you and the carrier.

At pickup, you and the driver complete a detailed condition report together. This document captures every existing ding, chip, scratch, and area of wear on the vehicle. Both parties sign it, and it becomes the baseline for the shipment. Take date-stamped photos at this stage as a backup to the written report.

Upon delivery in Maryland or at your destination, repeat a careful walk-around inspection. Compare the car to the original report, checking every panel, the windshield, mirrors, wheels, and undercarriage. If you notice any new damage that was clearly not present at pickup, document it immediately and note it on the bill of lading before the driver leaves.

Report concerns to Ship Guy right away. Car shipping companies provide liability insurance for transported vehicles, but filing a successful claim requires clear documentation of new damage, including photos, timestamps, and a signed condition report. Adequate insurance coverage for vehicles should be confirmed before shipping, so ask about the carrier's policy limits during the booking process.

Sign the final delivery paperwork and retain copies of all documents, including the bill of lading and the condition report. These records are essential for any potential insurance claim and serve as your proof of the vehicle's condition at both ends of the trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you ship.

How much does it cost to ship a vehicle?
Your price depends on the distance, vehicle size, transport type (open or enclosed), and how quickly you need it. We give you an upfront, all-in quote with no hidden fees — the number you see is the number you pay.
How long will my shipment take?
Transit time depends on the route and distance. Once your vehicle is dispatched, we share an estimated delivery window and keep you updated from pickup to drop-off. Longer cross-country routes naturally take more time than regional moves.
Is my vehicle insured during transport?
Yes. We only work with licensed, vetted carriers that carry active cargo insurance, and your vehicle is covered while it's on the truck. We're happy to share coverage details before you book.
What's the difference between open and enclosed transport?
Open transport is the most common and most affordable option and is safe for everyday vehicles. Enclosed transport adds extra protection from weather and road debris — a great choice for classic, luxury, or high-value vehicles.
Do I have to pay a deposit upfront?
No. You don't pay until your vehicle is assigned and dispatched to a carrier — so there's no risk in getting a quote and booking your shipment.
Can you ship a vehicle that doesn't run?
Yes. We transport both operable and inoperable (non-running) vehicles — just let us know when you book so we assign a carrier with the right equipment, such as a winch.
