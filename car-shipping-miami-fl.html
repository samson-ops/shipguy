Miami is one of the busiest auto transport markets in the country. Vehicles move in and out of South Florida year-round for relocations, seasonal residents, dealership and auction purchases, and college and military moves.

Quick answerShipping a standard sedan to or from Miami typically costs $450–$1,600 on open transport depending on distance, with pickup usually within 1–5 days of booking and delivery in 1–10 days by route. Enclosed transport runs roughly 40–60% more. During snowbird season (Oct–Dec southbound, Apr–May northbound), rates in the peak direction rise 20–40% — get an exact price for your route in about two minutes.

Ship Guy arranges car shipping to and from Miami and the surrounding Florida metro area. We match your vehicle with a vetted, insured carrier running your route, coordinate a pickup window that works for your schedule, and stay with the shipment through final delivery.

Because South Florida is a seasonal market, timing affects both price and availability. Rates and carrier supply shift with snowbird demand, so the earlier you book around those peak windows, the more flexibility you generally have.

What Shapes Your Miami Auto Transport Price

Five factors set most of your quote: distance and route popularity, vehicle size and weight, open vs. enclosed transport, season, and date flexibility. The I-95 corridor is Florida's cheapest lane per mile because carriers run it constantly — and if your pickup window can flex by two or three days, we can usually place your vehicle at a lower rate than a guaranteed-date booking.

Southbound peak • Oct–Dec

Seasonal residents ship cars into South Florida ahead of winter. Southbound rates rise 20–40%, while northbound trucks leave half-empty at a discount.

Northbound peak • Apr–May

The migration reverses in spring. Northbound prices spike as vehicles head home, and southbound becomes the bargain direction.

What it means for you: shipping against the seasonal flow often costs well under the averages above. Shipping with it? Book 2–3 weeks ahead — waiting until the week you fly out is the most expensive mistake snowbirds make. See our snowbird shipping guide →

Condo or high-rise delivery?

Many Miami buildings prohibit commercial trucks in garages and porte-cochères, and a 75-foot carrier can't fit down every street in Brickell or Miami Beach. Hurricane season (June–November)

Atlantic storms occasionally delay pickups a day or two as carriers reroute, and we never dispatch into an evacuation zone. When a named storm approaches South Florida we communicate proactively about timing — and if you're shipping a vehicle out ahead of a storm, book immediately, because outbound capacity disappears fast. Shipping overseas via PortMiami or Port Everglades? Tell us up front and we'll deliver directly to your freight forwarder or port warehouse.

Miami Car Shipping FAQ

Common Questions

How much does it cost to ship a car to or from Miami?
For a standard sedan on open transport, typical ranges are $450–$700 to the Southeast, $700–$1,100 to the Northeast or Midwest, and $1,100–$1,600 cross-country. Enclosed transport runs roughly 40–60% more. Your exact price depends on vehicle size, address accessibility, and season — South Florida rates move with snowbird demand. The fastest way to get an accurate number is to request a free quote.
How long does it take to ship a car to Miami?
Transit time depends mainly on distance. A move from Atlanta typically runs a couple of days, the Northeast is usually in the three-to-six day range, and a cross-country trip from the West Coast generally takes about a week or more. Weather, traffic, and route availability can shift these estimates.
Do you offer door-to-door pickup in Miami?
Yes. Whenever possible we arrange door-to-door pickup and delivery at your Miami address. Some areas — narrow streets in Coconut Grove or Little Havana, gated communities, and low-clearance parking garages downtown or in Brickell — can't safely fit a full-size carrier, so the driver will arrange a nearby meeting point such as a large lot.
When is the busiest season for car shipping in South Florida?
Demand peaks around the snowbird season. Vehicles move south into Florida heading into the fall and winter, and back north in the spring. Booking earlier during those windows generally means better pricing and more scheduling flexibility. See our snowbird car shipping page for details.
Can you ship a car that doesn't run?
Yes, but the carrier needs to know in advance. A non-running vehicle requires equipment such as a winch and a driver prepared to load it, so mention it when you request your quote and we'll match you with a carrier set up for inoperable vehicles.
Do you ship anything besides cars in Miami?
Yes. Along with vehicles, Ship Guy arranges transport for motorcycles, boats, freight and pallets, furniture, household goods, heavy equipment, and oversized items to and from the Miami area.
Can I put personal items in the car?
Most carriers allow up to about 100 lbs of items packed in the trunk or below window level. Personal items aren't covered by the carrier's cargo insurance, so never ship valuables, documents, or anything irreplaceable inside the vehicle.
Do I need to be present for pickup or delivery?
No. Any adult you authorize — a family member, friend, or building manager — can release or accept the vehicle and sign the inspection paperwork, which is why many snowbird customers ship their car a week before or after they fly.
Is my vehicle insured during transport?
Yes. The assigned carrier's cargo insurance covers your vehicle in transit, and the driver documents its condition with photos on the Bill of Lading at pickup and delivery. You can request the carrier's insurance certificate before pickup, and your own auto policy typically remains secondary coverage.
