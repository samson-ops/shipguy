Transport Options

Open vs. Enclosed Car Shipping

Both methods are safe — they simply serve different needs, and the cost difference is meaningful. Here is how to choose.

Enclosed transport

Open transport is the most common, most affordable way to ship — the same multi-car trailers you see carrying new cars to dealerships. It works well for sedans, SUVs, minivans, and daily-driver trucks, with wide route availability.

Enclosed transport places your vehicle inside a covered trailer that shields it from weather, road debris, salt spray, and windblown dust. It is the right call for luxury, classic, low-clearance, and freshly restored vehicles. Capacity is limited, so it usually costs 30–50% more and books a little further out.

Open Transport

Most popular & most affordable

Loaded onto multi-car open trailers with the widest route availability — the reliable, budget-friendly choice for standard vehicles.

  • Lowest cost per shipment
  • The most carriers and routes
  • Great for everyday sedans, SUVs & trucks
  • Faster to schedule

Enclosed Transport

Maximum protection

A fully covered trailer that guards against weather, debris, and Gulf Coast salt spray — built for vehicles you can't easily replace.

  • Weather & road-debris protection
  • Ideal for luxury, classic & exotic cars
  • Best for fresh paint or restorations
  • ~30–50% premium · book early

Shipping during hurricane season?

Gulf Coast storm season runs roughly June through November. Enclosed transport adds protection from severe weather and road salt during those months. Either way, we show you both prices side by side so you can decide what fits your budget.

Before Pickup

Preparing Your Vehicle for Shipping

Good preparation protects you and the carrier and helps avoid delays. Set aside 30–45 minutes before your scheduled pickup.

Remove personal items

Take out belongings, cables, garage openers, and trunk contents. Remove or deactivate toll tags to avoid surprise billing.

Remove accessories

Detach roof racks, bike mounts, spoilers, and antenna toppers — anything that could come loose or extends past the vehicle.

Wash & photograph

A clean car makes existing damage easy to document. Photograph all four sides, the corners, roof, wheels, and any prior marks.

Quarter tank of fuel

Enough for the driver to load and unload, light enough to avoid extra weight — the standard industry recommendation.

Mechanical basics

Check the parking brake, battery, and tire pressure, and disable alarms. Tell us at booking if the vehicle is inoperable.

Provide keys

The driver needs at least one working set — plus any gate codes or parking permits needed at the delivery location.
